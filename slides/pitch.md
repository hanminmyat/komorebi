# Who's my person?

The **family archivist** — a grandchild, adult child, or anyone holding the phone while grandma tells a story.

Not the storyteller. The one capturing it.
<!-- 20s -->

---

<!-- slide 2 -->
# Their problem

Families don't lose photos. They lose **context**.

- Take photos → store → forget
- Record audio → never revisit
- Stories behind objects disappear
- Reasons events mattered fade away

Family history slowly vanishes.
<!-- 20s -->

# What I built

**Komorebi** — 木漏れ日 — sunlight filtering through leaves.

A memory capsule app:
- 🎙️ Record voice stories directly
- 📷 Attach photos that hold meaning
- 📦 One capsule = one structured memory
- 🔁 Record → upload → preserve → rediscover

Turn family stories into living memories.

We're building a **memory structuring system**, not a media platform.

The next generation won't ask *"what was this photo?"*
